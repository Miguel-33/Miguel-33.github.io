Class Description

This family mediation training program will consist of lectures, written exercises, group discussions, and mediation simulations. It is a 46-hour class comprising 2 consecutive weekends (Friday, Saturday, and Sunday). Each participant will be involved in role-play exercises acting as the sole mediator, plaintiff, defendant, or attorney. During role play as a mediator, participants will be required to deliver an opening statement and mediate disputes to a resolution. The objective of role-play is to develop confidence and practical experience in the role of a mediator.

Training Covers:

  • Communication Skills
  • Community Resources and Referral Process
  • Court Process
  • Diversity Issues
  • Financial Issues
  • Tennessee Family Law
  • Children's Needs in Divorce Context
  • Mediation Process and Techniques
  • Psychological Issues in Divorce and Family Dynamics
  • Standards of Conduct for Mediators
  • And more...
