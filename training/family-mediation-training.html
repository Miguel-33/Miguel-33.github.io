Family Mediation Training Course

Our Family Mediation Training Program is designed for individuals seeking in-depth knowledge and skills in family mediation. This 46-hour program, delivered over two consecutive weekends (Friday, Saturday, and Sunday), combines theoretical learning with practical exercises, including lectures, group discussions, and simulated mediation scenarios. Participants gain the expertise needed to effectively mediate family disputes with a focus on resolving conflicts, supporting family dynamics, and understanding the complexities of family law.

Class Description

Throughout the training, participants will practice the role of mediator and other involved parties, including plaintiff, defendant, and attorney, in various scenarios. As mediators, participants will deliver opening statements and work to facilitate resolutions, building practical skills and confidence in family mediation.

Training Covers:

  • Custody and Visitation Issues in Domestic Violence Cases
  • Safety Planning and Risk Assessments
  • Recognizing Signs and Symptoms of Abuse
  • Understanding Police and Court System Responses to Domestic Violence
  • Barriers to Prosecution and Legal Challenges
  • Community Resources and Support Referrals
  • Protective Orders and Their Application
  • Confidentiality and Ethical Considerations
  • Criminal Offenses Related to Domestic Violence
  • Techniques for Managing Anger in Mediation
  • Maintaining Control During Mediation Sessions
  • The Role of Attorneys in Domestic Violence Mediation
  • And more...
